WELCOME TO SOUTH KOREA

PLACES TO GO

Gyeonghuigung Palace

Once a massive complex with 1,500 buildings and residence for kings for over 200 years.

Seoul Forest

Known for its cherry blossoms & tulips in Spring, Ginkgo Tree Forest in Autumn & deer.

Lotte World

Include the World's largest Indoor theme park & an outdoor Magic Island.

Suwon Hwaseong Fortress

UNESCO World Heritage with impressive structure from Joseon Dynasty.

Daegwallyeong: Yongpyong Ski Resort

The largest ski resort in Korea & host of 2018 Pyeongchang Winter Olympic Games.

Gwangalli Beach

Enjoy view of Gwangandaegyo Bridge & Gwangalli M Drone Light Show every Saturday.

Daewangam Park

A seaside park, known for its beautiful coastlines and interestingly-shaped rocks.

Bulguksa Temple & Seokguram Grotto

The first historical sites in Korea registered as UNESCO World Heritage.

Jeonju Hanok Village

The village is famous because of its traditional buildings that strongly contrast with the modern city around it.

Naejangsan National Park

A favourite autumn foliage destination with a beautiful temple in the park.

Cheongpung Lakeside Cable Car

Panoramic view of Cheongpung Lake on the 2.3km cable car ride, connected to Mt. Bibongsan.

Seoullo7017

A highway, now a beautiful sky garden walkway for pedestrians..

Windy Hill

A hill overlooking the sea & the location of many famous Korean dramas.

Tongyeong Skyline Luge

Ride the Skyride to get on board the Luge cart for exhilarating ride downhill.

High1 Ski Resort

One of the most popular ski resorts in Korea, known for its puffy snow.

Pocheon Art Valley

Experience the beauty of the quarry at this famous drama filming location.

Jebudo Island

Jebu Island, a mysterious island where sea routes open twice a day due to tides.

Myeongdong

Well-known shopping, dining & street food paradise. Where people can enjoy, and relax.

ABOUT

    South Korea, officially known as the Republic of Korea, is a country in East Asia constituting the southern part of the Korean Peninsula. It shares a border with North Korea and is surrounded by the sea on three sides. South Korea is known for its vibrant culture, rapid technological advancements, and rich history. The country boasts beautiful landscapes, from the bustling city life of Seoul to the serene beauty of its temples and natural parks. South Korea is also renowned for its pop culture, particularly K-pop and Korean dramas, which have garnered international fame.

The country has a fascinating blend of tradition and modernity, with ancient palaces and temples standing alongside futuristic skyscrapers. South Korea is also famous for its delectable cuisine, which includes iconic dishes such as Kimchi, Bibimbap, and Korean BBQ. South Korea's tourism industry continues to thrive, with millions of visitors every year eager to explore its cultural heritage, scenic beauty, and unique experiences.

Whether you're visiting for its historical landmarks, natural wonders, or urban excitement, South Korea has something to offer for every type of traveler.
